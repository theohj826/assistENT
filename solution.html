The Solution

Individuals with restricted nasal breathing need a comfortable and discreet way to counteract airway obstruction and increase nasal airflow without surgery. In order to address this need, we have developed Schnozzle, an internal nasal dilator that is inserted into obstructed nostrils to open the airways and aid breathing. Schnozzle’s use case is analogous to a contact lens; rather than undergoing surgery to correct nasal breathing, patients will simply deploy the device in nose to instantly improve breathing.

Schnozzle is a comfortable and discreet nasal dilator with multiple applications across a range of consumer health and clinical settings – daily use, sleep, and exercise. The device rests entirely within the nasal passage, where it dilates the nostrils to directly counteract nasal valve collapse and increase airflow. It is created with form-fitting materials that evenly distribute forces to reduce pressure on the nasal passage and enhance comfort. Furthermore, the device is able to remain stable entirely within the nasal passage without the external clamps employed by existing nasal dilators. Finally, the device is compatible with an intuitive applicator that ensures correct placement and safe removal.

Our design is informed by a diagnostic test performed by ENTs known as the modified Cottle maneuver, in which the physician pushes a Q-tip outward against the internal nasal valve, the flow-limiting segment within the nose [13]. This maneuver temporarily relieves symptoms of obstruction in 89% of patients [14]. While the technique is currently used diagnostically to determine whether patients are candidates for surgery, Schnozzle works by emulating this maneuver to directly counteract nasal valve collapse non-surgically. By targeting the internal nasal valve, Schnozzle achieves maximal airflow improvement with minimal dilation.
