The Problem

Restricted nasal breathing is one of the most common complaints heard by otolaryngologists (ENTs) every day [1]. In fact, 40 million Americans, approximately 13% of the total population, experience nasal obstruction and suffer labored nasal breathing during the day [13]. This symptom is indicative of nasal obstruction, which is often the result of a narrowing or collapse of the internal nasal valve [3]. Nasal obstruction is a daily source of discomfort that reduces productivity and quality of life [4]. Patients complain of trouble breathing throughout the day, problems sleeping and habitual snoring, and difficulty breathing during physical activity and exercise [4].

Reconstructive surgery is a common treatment for this condition. This involves using cartilage grafts taken from the ears or ribs to reconfigure the geometry of the nasal passages and thereby decrease resistance to airflow [5]. However, positive results are not guaranteed, and up to 20% of patients experience worsened symptoms postoperatively [7]. Moreover, this surgery is invasive, involving cartilage grafts taken from the ribs or ears, and it entails a four-week recovery period [5]. Even so, surgeons perform over 125,000 functional rhinoplasty procedures each year in the United States [22]. And at an average cost of $5,000, nasal obstruction imposes a surgical burden of $625M to the healthcare system annually [22]

Alternatively, sufferers may use over-the-counter nasal dilators, such as Breathe Right strips, which are intended to temporarily manage nasal obstruction by non-surgically widening the nasal passages [8]. However, based on thorough analysis of online product reviews, observations shadowing ENTs in clinic, and dozens of patient interviews, we are confident that existing devices are unacceptable for daily use. Prevailing dilators include visible external portions that carry social stigma if worn in public. Moreover, they exert forces on the nose that are not tolerable for prolonged use. They are also difficult to apply and position correctly and easily become dislodged.

There is an incredible opportunity for AssistENT to be the first device on the market available specifically for daily use.
